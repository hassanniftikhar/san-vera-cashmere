01 — Where We Are

The Situation: ~700 Units, 12 Weeks, Clear Objective

San Vera has approximately 700 units sitting in FBA. With the summer season now in full swing and a sell-through deadline of end of July 2026, we have a focused 12-week window to move all inventory efficiently. Price is confirmed at $39.99 — a strong, competitive position in the mid-market that gives us real conversion potential without racing to the bottom.

The good news: summer dress demand peaks from May through July — we are entering the market at exactly the right moment. The strategy is built around three tightly scoped phases: building velocity in May, scaling hard through June, and closing out remaining stock in July. Every campaign decision flows from this single objective.

The Numbers Behind the Plan
At $39.99 with ~700 units, total potential revenue is ~$28,000. To sell through by end of July we need to move approximately 58 units per week — roughly 8–9 units per day across all variations. That is an achievable target with focused PPC, the right keyword strategy, and the summer season working in our favour. The plan below is designed specifically around this number.
01
Key Learning
Feature-Specific Keywords Are Our Strongest Signal
The clearest data point from our launch campaigns: "smocked summer dress" delivered 22% ACOS — by far the best-performing keyword in the account. Specific, feature-based terms where San Vera's product is a natural fit convert at a much higher rate than broad category terms.

This insight is the cornerstone of our keyword strategy. We lead with what we know works, then layer in broader terms as the listing builds conversion history.
02
Market Timing
Peak Season Is Now — The Window Is Open
Summer dress search volume peaks between May and July. "Summer dresses for women 2026" is showing +1,048% growth in search trend right now. This means our PPC spend will get more impressions per dollar in the next 12 weeks than at any other point in the year.

Timing is a genuine advantage here. The market is coming to us — our job is to make sure San Vera is visible when shoppers are looking.
03
Inventory Logic
$39.99 Is the Right Price for This Strategy
At $39.99, San Vera sits meaningfully above the cheap fast-fashion segment ($15–$25) while remaining accessible to the mainstream summer shopper. It positions the product as a quality choice rather than a clearance item.

Break-even ACOS at $39.99 is ~44%. Our Phase 2 and Phase 3 targets are designed to reach or surpass that, meaning the majority of inventory sold in the back half of this plan will be sold profitably.
04
Opportunity
Auto Campaign Is Already Working — We Scale From Here
The automatic campaign's close-match and complements targeting has been generating real purchases at 52–55% ACOS — solid performance for a listing still building its review base. We don't need to rebuild from scratch.

The refined strategy concentrates budget on what's already converting, adds the feature keyword exact match layer on top, and systematically expands reach as the listing's conversion data strengthens.

06 — Phased Roadmap

A Clear Three-Phase Path to Profitable Growth

Why Three Phases and Not One Big Push?
Dumping the entire budget into week one would generate clicks but not necessarily sales — a new listing needs a few weeks of consistent purchase signals before Amazon's algorithm starts ranking it organically for broader terms. Phase 1 builds that foundation. Phase 2 capitalises on it at exactly the moment summer demand peaks. Phase 3 converts the organic momentum into a final clearance sprint. It is a sequence built for this specific 12-week window.
Phase 1
Velocity Build — Establish Sales Rhythm (May 8 → June 1)

3.5 weeks to establish a consistent daily sales rate and feed the Amazon algorithm with purchase signals. The goal is to reach a steady 8–10 units per day by end of May. Campaigns are tight and data-focused — every week we harvest converting search terms from auto and sharpen the manual campaign around what works.

  • Price confirmed at $39.99 — all variations updated and live
  • Auto campaign (close-match + complements) running at $100–120/day
  • Manual Exact (feature keywords) running at $120–150/day
  • Target: sell 100–160 units by June 1
  • Search term report reviewed weekly — converting terms moved to manual, negatives added continuously
  • End of phase: assess top-selling variations (Sky Blue S/M, Ivory S historically strongest) and increase bids on those SKUs
  • ACOS of 60–70% is expected and appropriate — we are buying velocity and algorithm trust
